Privacy Policy

Last Updated: May 1, 2026

JKAPPs ("we," "our," or "us") operates the WORDaGRAM: Neon Edition mobile application. This policy details how data is handled by our local game systems and the third-party Google services we utilize.

1. Third-Party Services & Data Processing

While JKAPPs does not operate any private servers to collect your personal information, we use Google and Firebase services for game optimization and advertising. These services process data on Google's secure infrastructure:

  • Google AdMob & UMP: Collects device identifiers to manage user consent and serve advertisements.
  • Firebase Analytics: Collects anonymized performance events (such as round starts and app usage statistics). This data is aggregated and does not identify you personally.
  • Firebase Crashlytics: Collects technical logs and stack traces when the app encounters an error to help us provide updates and bug fixes.

2. User Choices and Opt-Outs

We provide the following methods to manage your privacy:

  • Consent Management: A consent dialog (Google UMP) is presented at launch for users in regulated regions to choose their ad preferences.
  • System Settings: You can reset your Advertising ID or opt out of personalized ads at any time via your device:
    Settings > Google > Ads.
  • Data Deletion: Because your personal game data is stored only on your device, you can delete all such data by clearing the app's cache or uninstalling the app.

3. On-Device Local Storage

The following data is stored locally and exclusively on your device’s internal storage:

  • Individual high scores and player initials.
  • Daily check-in streak progress.
  • Game session history.

This specific player achievement data is never transmitted to JKAPPs or any external servers. It remains on your device to facilitate the offline leaderboard and streak features.

4. Assets and Licenses

Audio: All sound effects are programmatically synthesized and are original content.

Dictionaries: English word lists are provided via open-source projects including SCOWL, Hunspell, dwyl, and the English Open Word List (EOWL) under their respective licenses.

5. Security

We rely on the security protocols of Google and Firebase to protect any data processed by their third-party services. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure.
